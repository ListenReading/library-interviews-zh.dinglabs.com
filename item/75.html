China, Cheng du

The first step in the negotiation process is to investigate the other company. Now, Chinese companies, when looking into the other company, they generally will first put together a small group internally and make it this group’s responsibility to do the investigating. After that, this group (or team) will typically look into and research public market information on the company, if such information is available. Also, they may go through a separate agent company. For example, there are certain independent companies that do market research and investigation, or something like a detective company will gain information of the other company’s credit, its suppliers, and its marketing situation. As far as consulting with Americans is concerned, will we or will we not alter this procedure? I think we certainly will alter it. First of all, it’s because if the other company is a U.S. company, then when the Chinese company is investigating them, they will run into certain obstacles. For instance, they may be unable to gather any information, or unable to gather all of the information that is required. So, the Chinese company will perhaps have to hire a foreign credit check agency to do the job. Or they may have to adopt still other methods.谈判的第一程序，就是说，调查对方公司。那中国公司通常在调查对方公司的时候，首先会在内部成立一个小组，由这个小组负责去调查。然后，这个小组，调查的时候，他们通常会，如果有公开市场信息的话，他们会去研究对方公司的公开市场信息。另外，他们也可能会，通过其它的中介公司。比如说，嗯，有一些市场上独立的市场调查研究公司，或者是类似于侦探公司去了解对方公司的信用，它的供应商，它的销售情况。嗯，至于，对美国人协商的时候，是不是要更改这个程序？我认为，一定会更改。首先是因为，如果对方是美国公司，那，中国公司，他们在搜集别人信息的时候，会遇到，嗯，某些障碍。比如说，它无法搜集到，可能它无法搜集到它完全需要的这些信息。那么，这些公司有可能会委托海外信用调查机构去调查。也可能，嗯，采取一些别的办法。
