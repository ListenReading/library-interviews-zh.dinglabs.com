The Chinese like to play chess. That also means, in making changes to things that are long-range, or tactical, or strategic, their minds do not work as quickly as Americans…they cannot transform as readily. Another thing is, they might prepare 1, 2, or 3 possible plans, but if they go beyond the scope of those plans, they’ll sometimes have to get instructions from their bosses. This is because you have to involve the upper divisions, etc. Sometimes this makes you late and you have to put off your answer for a while. Sometimes compromises can’t be made either because they often don’t have the ability (i.e. authority) to make an immediate decision. So, on this point we can’t compare with the agility of the Americans.中国人喜欢下棋，也就是说，中国人呢，做一般比较长远的，战略上的，战术上的临时改变呢，哦，没有美国人的脑子这么快，这么能够改变。啊。还有一个呢，就是说，他们可能会准备一、二、三套方案。但是如果超过这个范围以外呢，他有时候要去请示他们的领导啊，因为要涉及到上级部门啊等等。所以呢，有时候会晚，来推迟一下这个回答。有些让步呢，也不能。因为他们有时候不能马上个人来做这个决定的主张。所以，在这些方面呢，是比不上美国这么灵活。
