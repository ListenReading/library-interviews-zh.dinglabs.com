Um, does male chauvinism exist in China…. We first need to establish a definition for male chauvinism. Perhaps some people might say, um, that men prefer to control everything. For example, they, they, everything has to be done according to their wishes, and not yours… [things are] not done through negotiation and with consideration for the good of others, especially in the home. This trend… perhaps… this is so-called male chauvinism. But the situation should also be broken down geographically. For example, in some more backward places in the north, the culture isn’t very developed. They, the traditional thinking that men are important and women are inferior is still very prevalent. In this environment, if, for example, they’re having children, they might always want a boy. And the men think that women should serve them. In… This is a result of their education; the education they’ve gotten is more backward. But in the more economically developed cities in the south, the situation is completely different. In the south, most births are planned. Families can only have one child, so the ratio of boys to girls and their relative importance and their level of education are comparable. In this situation, boys and girls have the same educational opportunities and now girls’ grades at school are generally better than boys’. So, uh, boys, girls actually hold a very important place in today’s society. Some women… many women are, are very successful at work. They can be CEOs or CFOs or hold other high positions. Um, this, if [we’re talking about] economic independence, in the south, in these places, male chauvinism can’t really emerge.
