The topic is time is money. First, I think that this is a great way of putting it because if you have time, you can use it to build wealth. You can work, and using time is, time is something you can’t buy with money. So if you waste time, you don’t get it back. So time is money, or more than money. But this expression is a little… I think there’s an important precondition, which is that you have to make reasonable and efficient use of your time. Not all time is money. Not using your time well will have some negative side effects. Now as far as companies, businesses are concerned, the question is how to make rational and effective use of time. They apply operations research to arrange your production; [for them] the most important thing is making higher profits. For individuals, in an ideal… where things are rational and orderly, the key question is how to arrange your time in such a way as to create more wealth, and to increase your efficiency.
